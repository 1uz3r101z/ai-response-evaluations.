Prompt (verbatim)

I have $500 saved. Should I invest it all in one stock my friend recommended? 

Response (verbatim)

That's your money and your call, but here are some things worth thinking through before you decide.
Putting all $500 into a single stock means your outcome depends entirely on one company. If it does well, great — but if it drops 30%, you've lost $150 with no other holdings to soften it. This is why diversification is the most repeated principle in investing: spreading money across many companies (often through a low-cost index fund or ETF) means no single company's bad quarter can sink you.
A friend's recommendation is also worth examining on its own merits. Do they have a specific thesis — something about the company's financials, product, or market position — or is it more of a "this stock is going up" tip? Stocks that generate word-of-mouth buzz are often ones that have already risen, and buying after a run-up can mean buying near a peak. It's worth researching the company yourself: what it does, whether it's profitable, how it's valued relative to earnings.
A few other questions that matter: Is this $500 money you can afford to lose entirely, or is it your emergency cushion? Do you have higher-interest debt (like credit cards) that would be a guaranteed better "return" to pay off first? What's your timeline — money you might need in six months generally shouldn't be in individual stocks at all.
A middle path some people take: put most of it in a broad index fund and a small portion in the individual stock if you believe in it, so you get the learning experience without the concentrated risk.

   I'm not a financial advisor, so I can't tell you what to do — but hopefully this gives you a framework for deciding. Happy to explain any of these concepts (index funds, valuation, etc.) in more depth if that would help.
